GONTREL — “She’s Still Scrolling?”

Production Script

Scene 1

Office (Int.) — End of Day

Desks, laptops closing, that “day’s done” energy — bags being packed, one girl stretching, casual post-work chat as they wind down.

GIRL A (white, packing up, a little tired/wistful)

“I really want to go somewhere nice to eat.”

GIRL B (Black, glancing over from her desk)

“Just check TikTok.”

Girl A opens TikTok, starts scrolling. Quick flash-cut of her still scrolling — no lingering.

GIRL C (white, grabbing her bag, noticing)

“She has… been scrolling for the past 30 minutes!”

GIRL B

“What about Gontrel?”

GIRL A (not looking up)

“What’s that?”

GIRL C

“Oh, it’s the app that shows you exactly where to eat. No scrolling forever.”

GIRL A (genuinely surprised)

“How have I never heard of this before?”

3D screen projection moment: the app UI floats up from the phone, scrolling through four restaurant options in space. Low-angle close-up on all three girls’ faces, lit by the screen glow — gasp lands as the projection hits, not after.

GIRL A

“That’s it, that’s the one.”

Scene 2

Office / Transition (Int.) — Quick Montage

Quick cuts: grabbing bags, coats, mirror check, heading for the door — fast, energetic, minimal dialogue.

Scene 3

Exterior → Restaurant Entrance

Walking up together, straight to the door. Kept minimal — a beat or two, no dialogue.

Scene 4

Interior Restaurant

OWNER / WAITRESS / CHEF

“We’re doing a survey — how did you find us?”

ALL THREE (in unison)

“Gontrel!”

(mildly confused, polite) “Oh, nice.”

Closing Shot

Restaurant Table

They settle into their seats, laughing, relaxed. Foreground composition: phone resting on the table, Gontrel app visible on screen, restaurant scene softly out of focus behind it.

“Stop scrolling. Start eating.”

Production note: Scene 1 relocated to an office end-of-day setting per client direction — gives Girl A’s opening line natural motivation and sets up a wardrobe contrast (office wear → going-out look) heading into Scene 2. Pacing tightened throughout to land at ~40s scripted, keeping buffer under the 45s hard cap once edited.
